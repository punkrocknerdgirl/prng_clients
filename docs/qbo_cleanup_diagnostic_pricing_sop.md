# QBO Cleanup Diagnostic & Pricing SOP

## Purpose
Use this process to review a prospective cleanup client’s QuickBooks Online file, define the actual scope, and produce one fixed-fee quote that matches the work required.

Do not price from transaction count alone. Price from the combination of date range, reconciliation condition, balance sheet risk, transaction cleanup volume, missing support, and uncertainty.

---

## 1. Define the review period

Record:

- Cleanup start date
- Cleanup end date
- Number of months in scope
- Whether the end date is a fixed historical date or “current”
- Whether books must be brought fully current before handoff

If the quote says “through current,” update the ending period and price before sending whenever additional months have passed since the diagnostic review.

---

## 2. Gather access and source documents

Minimum review access:

- Accountant access to QBO
- Bank and credit card statements for the review period, when available
- Loan and line-of-credit statements
- Payroll reports if payroll liabilities or payroll clearing accounts exist
- Prior-year tax returns or adjusting entries when needed to identify opening balances

Missing records increase uncertainty and may require a contingency, exclusion, or later change order.

---

## 3. Review QBO in this order

### A. Banking and reconciliations

Review every bank, savings, credit card, loan, and line-of-credit account.

Document:

- Last reconciled date
- Reconciliation status by account
- Beginning-balance discrepancies
- Reconciliation adjustments
- Uncleared checks, payments, deposits, and transfers
- Stale transactions from prior periods
- Duplicate transactions
- Negative bank, credit card, loan, or line-of-credit balances
- Cash or reserve accounts that may contain misposted activity

Key scope question:

> Are reconciliations trustworthy, repairable, or unusable?

A technically reconciled account can still require major cleanup if stale or duplicate activity remains in the register.

### B. Transaction review

Review transaction volume and condition across the full scope period.

Document:

- Uncategorized income and expenses
- Missing payees
- Transactions posted to Ask My Accountant or similar holding accounts
- Personal or owner activity mixed with business expenses
- Duplicate or deleted/recreated transactions
- Inconsistent coding across years
- Bank-feed transactions still awaiting review
- Suspense, clearing, or temporary accounts still carrying balances

### C. Chart of accounts

Document:

- Duplicate accounts
- Accounts with incorrect types or detail types
- Unused or placeholder accounts
- Multiple accounts used for the same purpose
- Contractor, payroll, owner, loan, or inventory activity split inconsistently
- Old deleted accounts still carrying balances

### D. Balance Sheet

Review every balance sheet account, not just cash.

Document:

- Opening Balance Equity
- Negative liabilities
- Inventory or fixed assets that do not exist
- Loan and line-of-credit balances
- Payroll and benefit liabilities
- Sales tax payable
- Customer deposits and retainers
- Undeposited Funds
- Unapplied cash payment accounts
- Clearing and cleanup accounts
- Owner equity, draws, contributions, and personal expenses
- Old balances unsupported by statements or source documents

Balance sheet uncertainty is one of the strongest pricing drivers.

### E. Accounts receivable

Review A/R Aging Summary and open invoices.

Document:

- Number and age of open invoices
- Duplicate invoices or payments
- Unapplied customer payments
- Credits and negative customer balances
- Invoices that require collection, write-off, or client confirmation

Separate bookkeeping cleanup from collection work.

### F. Accounts payable

Review A/P Aging Summary and open bills.

Document:

- Number and age of unpaid bills
- Duplicate bills or bill payments
- Vendor credits
- Negative vendor balances
- Bills that require payment, deletion, or client confirmation

### G. Profit and Loss

Review year by year and comparatively.

Document:

- Large swings or obvious anomalies
- Income posted to expense accounts or vice versa
- Contractor payments split across categories
- Payroll posted inconsistently
- Loan principal or transfers posted as income/expense
- Personal expenses
- Suspicious round-dollar or year-end entries
- Whether the P&L is mostly usable or requires reconstruction

### H. Supporting systems and special areas

When applicable, review:

- Payroll
- Sales tax
- Inventory
- Job costing
- Classes and locations
- Merchant processors
- Accounts connected to outside apps
- Loan amortization
- Fixed assets and depreciation
- 1099 contractor setup

## 4. Classify the cleanup

### Level 1: Limited cleanup

Typical condition:

- Current or recent period
- Reconciliations largely trustworthy
- Limited missing payees or miscoding
- Few balance sheet problems
- Source documents available

### Level 2: Targeted cleanup

Typical condition:

- Books may be reconciled but registers contain stale, duplicate, or unsupported items
- Multiple balance sheet accounts require correction
- Coding is inconsistent across months or years
- A/R or A/P issues are contained rather than systemwide
- P&L is usable after cleanup

### Level 3: Historical reconstruction

Typical condition:

- Multiple unreconciled years
- Broken beginning balances
- Missing statements or source documents
- Reconciliations must be undone and rebuilt
- Transactions must be recreated or tied to tax returns
- Significant payroll, sales tax, inventory, loan, or equity reconstruction

Do not quote Level 3 work as ordinary cleanup.

---

## 5. Price the work

Estimate the work by component rather than choosing a number from instinct alone.

For each component, estimate:

- Review and research time
- Correction time
- Client-question and follow-up time
- Reconciliation time
- Documentation and final review time
- Uncertainty buffer

Pricing drivers:

- Number of months and accounts
- Transaction volume
- Reconciliation condition
- Number and age of stale items
- Balance sheet complexity
- Payroll, sales tax, inventory, loans, or job costing
- Missing statements or support
- Client responsiveness
- Need to coordinate with a CPA or prior bookkeeper
- Risk of scope expansion

Use a fixed fee only after exclusions and assumptions are explicit.

Recommended payment structure for substantial cleanup:

- 50% to begin
- 50% at completion

For unusually large or uncertain projects, use milestone payments instead.

---

## 6. Build the quote

Unless the client specifically needs alternatives, provide one recommended scope and one price.

Include:

- Exact cleanup period
- Summary of major findings
- Work included
- Deliverables
- Expected timeline
- Client documents and access required
- Fixed fee and payment schedule
- Assumptions
- Exclusions
- Change-order language for new issues outside the reviewed scope

Common exclusions:

- Tax return preparation or amendment
- Audit or attestation work
- Legal or tax advice
- Collections
- Lender-specific reconstruction
- Unsupported historical balances that require outside research
- Ongoing bookkeeping after the stated cleanup period
- Additional months added after the quote’s stated ending date

---

## 7. Report structure

Create:

1. Executive Summary / Overview
2. Detailed Findings

Use the Detailed Findings header on every report page:

- Client and report title
- Prepared-for line and date
- Reporting-period badge
- PRNG Bookkeeping Services / Goldsmith Business Solutions badge

Keep client colors in CSS variables so the theme can be changed from one central location.

## 8. Dad’s Roofing reference examples

These are examples of how scope changed pricing. They are not permanent price standards.

### Version 1: 2024 only

- Period: January 1, 2024 through December 31, 2024
- Quote: $3,500
- Cleanup was limited to one historical year

### Version 2: 2024 through May 2026

- Period: January 1, 2024 through May 31, 2026
- Quote: $4,500
- Scope included additional years and current-period cleanup

Important:

The second quote ends in May 2026. If Dad’s Roofing requests cleanup beyond May 2026, review the additional months and issue a revised price. Do not reuse the $4,500 price without updating the scope.

---

## 9. Current prospective cleanup

Planned quote structure:

- One quote only
- Cleanup period: January 1, 2021 through current
- Final price to be determined after completing this diagnostic process

Do not create separate 2021-only and 2021-to-current options unless the QBO review reveals a practical reason to split the project.

---

## Final rule

Price the work required to make the books supportable and usable. Do not price based only on how tidy the dashboard looks, how large the balances are, or how confident the client sounds.
